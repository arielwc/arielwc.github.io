Reflecting on my Learning

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

I want to be a developer, I want to create software that helps people, improve's their lives and contributes positively to society. I want to be continuously learning and growing and pushing myself.

Upon finishing and Dev Acadey, I hope to obtain a junior position in a development team at a company whose values align with mine, who are doing something meaninful. I want to continuously progress in my own learning while mentoring others. I want to always have my own projects on the go, building software that empowers people.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

My strengths will be my ability to focus and to work hard. My limitations will be my energy management and time management.

" My strengths lie in analysis, problem-solving, articulating and processing. I can think about the big picture and get lost in fine details, doing these things simultaneously can be difficult though."

I think these are still my main strengths, I am also good at persivering.

"My limitations would definitely include time-management and organisation. I tend to underestimate how long I’m going to need to do something. I tend to jump to saying “I can do this in 20 minutes”, whereas I should be saying “I could push myself to do this in 20 minutes, assuming I was fully rested, fed and caffeinated, had no distractions and all processes involved went swimmingly every step of the way, but life happens and I don’t want to stress myself out so it’s going to take an hour.”"

These are definitely still limitations of mine. The time-boxing has helped but I still tend to go over the time-box allocations if I get stuck. I havn't reached out when I've been stuck during foundations, I definitely will be doing this during the bootcamp, I think I will find this easier in person.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I am apprehensive about the pair-programming and the group projects. At times I find it draining to work closely with others, I enjoy it but it takes a lot more energy for me than working alone.

Speaking in front of the group will probably be difficult at least at first, but once I get to know people it should be ok.
What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

I would like to develope my time-management and organisational skills as well as my ability to work in groups.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?

I expect that I will put my best effort into everything that I do. I will do everything I can outside of the bootcamp to facilitate me putting my best into the bootcamp. I will keep up with my excercise routines and prioritise sleeping and eating well. I will let myself take the less stressful option in some things e.g. I will take an Uber if it will be less stressful than taking public transport in certain situations, I will have groceries delivered if it will be easier than going to the supermarket, I will buy two of something to give myself peace of mind that I won't run out, things that I usually would try to minimise but right now I will let myself have.
